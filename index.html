Getting Started

The Town of Enfield Interactive Map provides public access to property records, zoning information, school districts, flood zones, and more for all parcels within Enfield, CT.
How to find a property: Use the search bar in the top right to look up any parcel by street address, Parcel ID, or Map and Lot number. Clicking a result will zoom the map to that parcel and load its details in the left panel.
💡 New to the app? Use the on-screen tour (launched automatically on first visit) to get a guided walkthrough of the key features.
🔍

Search

The search bar at the top right accepts three types of input. Type your search term and select a result from the dropdown to zoom directly to that parcel.

Property Information Panel

When a parcel is selected, the left panel populates with detailed property information organized into collapsible sections:
Property — Parcel ID, PID, address
Ownership — Owner name, book/page
Land — Acreage, map number, zoning
Sales — Recent sale history
School Districts — Elementary and high school assignment
Sanitation & Winter Roads — Refuse pickup day, plow/sanding routes
Fire District — Assigned fire district with link
Flood Zone & FEMA — FEMA panel links and flood zone classification
💡 Action buttons at the top of the panel provide quick links to the Assessor Card, Google Map, Bing Map, Street View, Permitting, and Land Records.

Select Tool

The Select tool lets you click or draw on the map to select one or more parcels. Selected parcels will be highlighted and their details will load in the property panel.
  • 1
    Click the Select icon in the toolbar.
  • 2
    Use the dropdown arrow to switch between point click, rectangle draw, or polygon draw selection modes.
  • 3
    Click or draw on the map to select parcels. Multiple parcels can be selected at once.
  • 4
    Click Clear All to deselect all parcels.
💡 The Select tool defaults to the Parcels layer. Other selectable layers may be toggled in the panel.
✏️

Share

The Share tool generates a shareable link to the current map view. The link preserves your current zoom level, location, and selected parcel so recipients see exactly what you see.
  • 1
    Click the Share icon in the toolbar.
  • 2
    A shortened URL is generated automatically. Copy it to your clipboard.
  • 3
    Optionally share directly via Email, Facebook, X (Twitter), Pinterest, or LinkedIn.
  • 4
    Use the Embed option (the </> icon) to get an iframe code for embedding the map on a website.
📬

Abutters Mail Label Generator

The Mail Label Generator is a custom tool developed by the Town of Enfield GIS Division. It converts an abutters list exported from the Near Me / Abutters tool into print-ready Avery 5160 mailing labels.
  • 1
    Use the Abutters tool to generate a list of nearby parcels and export it as a ZIP file.
  • 2
    Open the Mail Label Generator from the toolbar.
  • 3
    Upload the exported ZIP file in Step 1 of the generator.
  • 4
    Configure your label options in Step 2.
  • 5
    Click Generate in Step 3 to download a print-ready PDF.
💡 This tool is intended for use with public hearing notifications and other mailings requiring abutting property owner lists.
📍

Abutters (Near Me)

The Abutters tool identifies parcels within a specified distance of a selected location. It is commonly used to generate lists of neighboring property owners for public hearing notifications.
  • 1
    Click the Abutters icon in the toolbar.
  • 2
    Choose your location method: click on map, draw a line, or draw a polygon.
  • 3
    Set your desired buffer distance in feet.
  • 4
    The tool will highlight all parcels within that distance and list their owners.
  • 5
    Export the list as a ZIP file for use with the Mail Label Generator.
📏

Measure

The Measure tool lets you calculate distances and areas directly on the map. Results display in real time as you click points.
  • 1
    Click the Measure icon in the toolbar.
  • 2
    Select distance or area measurement mode.
  • 3
    Choose your preferred units (Feet, Miles, Meters, etc.) from the dropdown.
  • 4
    Click on the map to place your first point, then continue clicking to add segments.
  • 5
    Double-click to finish. Use the trash icon to clear the measurement.
🖨️

Print

The Print tool exports the current map view as a printable document. First position and zoom the map to the area you want to print, then use this tool to generate the output.
  • 1
    Zoom and pan the map to the area you want to print.
  • 2
    Click the Print icon in the toolbar.
  • 3
    Select a Template (e.g., A3 Landscape).
  • 4
    Check Show print area to preview what will be included.
  • 5
    Click Print to generate the PDF. A new tab or download will open.
⚠️ If nothing happens when you click Print, check that your browser is not blocking pop-ups from this site. Allow pop-ups and try again.

FAQ & Troubleshooting

How do I find a specific property? ▼
Use the search bar at the top right. You can search by street address, Parcel ID, or Map and Lot number. If you're not getting results, try a shorter search term — for example, type "Hazard" instead of "100 Hazard Avenue."
Who owns this parcel? ▼
Select the parcel on the map or search for it by address. Ownership information appears in the Ownership section of the left-hand property panel.
Is this property in a flood zone? ▼
Select the parcel and scroll to the Flood Zone & FEMA Information section in the property panel. From there you can view or download the official FEMA FIRM map panel for that parcel.
What zoning district is this parcel in? ▼
Select the parcel and open the Zoning section in the property panel. The zone code is listed there. For full zoning regulations, contact the Town of Enfield Planning & Zoning department.
Can I use this data for a legal survey or boundary dispute? ▼
No. The data in this application is for informational and planning purposes only. Parcel boundaries shown are approximate. For official boundary determinations, consult a licensed land surveyor.
How accurate is the parcel data? ▼
Parcel data is provided by the Town of Enfield Assessor's Office and updated periodically. While efforts are made to maintain accuracy, the Town does not guarantee the data is free of errors. Always verify critical information with the Assessor's Office directly.
Why isn't the map loading or why is it slow? ▼
Try refreshing the page. The map works best in Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge. Avoid Internet Explorer. If issues persist, clear your browser cache or try a different browser.
Why don't certain layers display when zoomed in or out? ▼
Some layers are scale-dependent and only display at certain zoom levels. If a layer isn't showing, try zooming in or out a few levels. The Legend will indicate which layers are currently visible.
Nothing happens when I click Print. What do I do? ▼
Your browser may be blocking pop-ups from this site. Look for a pop-up blocked notification in your browser's address bar and click it to allow pop-ups from this application, then try printing again.
How do I zoom back out to see all of Enfield? ▼
Click the Home button (house icon) on the left side of the map to return to the default full-town extent.
The information shown for my property is incorrect. Who do I contact? ▼
Property data is sourced from the Town of Enfield Assessor's Office. To report an error, please contact the GIS Division or Assessor's Office directly using the contact information below.
How do I turn on aerial/satellite imagery? ▼
Click the Basemap Gallery or Local Aerial Gallery icons in the toolbar. Select Imagery or one of the Connecticut-specific aerial options such as 2023 Spring, Color, 3 inch for the highest resolution available.
